🧠 Base Pairing Rule — MEMORIZE THIS
A — T   |   G — C
Adenine pairs with Thymine (2 bonds) • Guanine pairs with Cytosine (3 bonds)
In RNA: A pairs with Uracil instead of Thymine!
🧬
DNA Structure
• Sugar-phosphate backbone
• Nitrogenous bases in middle
• Double helix shape
• Antiparallel strands (3'→5' & 5'→3')
• Found in nucleus
🔄
DNA Replication
• Happens during S phase
• Semiconservative — each new DNA has 1 old + 1 new strand
• Helicase unzips the helix
• DNA Polymerase builds new strand
• Goes 5'→3' direction only
📝
Gene vs Genome
• Gene = segment of DNA that codes for a protein
• Genome = ALL the DNA of an organism
• Humans have ~20,000-25,000 genes
• 46 chromosomes (23 pairs) in humans
⚙️ Protein Synthesis
🧫
TRANSCRIPTION: DNA → mRNA (happens in the NUCLEUS)
TRANSLATION: mRNA → Protein (happens at RIBOSOMES in cytoplasm)
Think: "TRANS-cription = making a TRANSCRIPT (copy)" | "TRANS-lation = TRANSLATING to protein language"
🔓 Transcription — DNA → mRNA
RNA Polymerase unwinds DNA → reads template strand → builds complementary mRNA using RNA nucleotides (A,U,G,C). The mRNA leaves the nucleus.
✂️ mRNA Processing (Eukaryotes only)
Introns (non-coding) are CUT OUT. Exons (coding) are KEPT and spliced together. 5' cap and poly-A tail added for protection.
🔤 Translation — mRNA → Protein
Ribosome reads mRNA in groups of 3 bases = CODONS. tRNA brings amino acids matching each codon. Amino acids link into a polypeptide chain (protein).
🔑 Start & Stop Codons
AUG = START codon (also codes for Methionine). UAA, UAG, UGA = STOP codons (no amino acid, ends translation).
🧠 Mnemonic: Where things happen
"DNA stays HOME (nucleus), mRNA TRAVELS to work (ribosome)"
Transcription = nucleus | Translation = ribosome (cytoplasm/rough ER)
🫛 Mendelian Genetics
📚
Key Vocabulary
• Allele — different versions of a gene
• Dominant (B) — uppercase, masks recessive
• Recessive (b) — lowercase, only shows when bb
• Homozygous — BB or bb (same alleles)
• Heterozygous — Bb (different alleles)
• Genotype — genetic makeup (Bb)
• Phenotype — physical appearance (brown eyes)
📜
Mendel's Laws
Law of Segregation:
Each organism has 2 alleles per trait. They separate during gamete formation (meiosis) — each gamete gets only 1 allele.

Law of Independent Assortment:
Genes on DIFFERENT chromosomes are inherited independently of each other.

Punnett Square — Monohybrid Cross (Tt × Tt)

🧩 Non-Mendelian Inheritance
🌸
Incomplete Dominance
Neither allele fully dominates → BLENDING

Red (RR) × White (WW) = PINK (RW)

Phenotypic ratio: 1:2:1
(1 red : 2 pink : 1 white)
🅰️
Codominance
BOTH alleles expressed simultaneously — no blending

Example: Blood type AB (both A and B antigens on red blood cells)

Roan cattle: Red AND white hairs visible
🩸
Multiple Alleles — ABO Blood Type
3 alleles: I^A, I^B, i
I^A and I^B are codominant
i is recessive

A = I^A I^A or I^A i
B = I^B I^B or I^B i
AB = I^A I^B
O = ii
🔗 Sex-Linked Traits & Mutations
👦
Why males get sex-linked diseases MORE often:
Males = XY → only ONE X chromosome. If that X has a recessive bad gene, no second X to mask it!
Females = XX → need BOTH X's to have the gene to show it. They're often carriers (X^n X).
Examples: Color blindness, Hemophilia, Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy
🧬
Chromosomal Mutations
Nondisjunction: chromosomes fail to separate during meiosis

• Trisomy 21 = Down Syndrome (3 copies of chr.21)
• Turner (XO) = only 1 sex chromosome
• Klinefelter (XXY) = extra X in males
✂️
Gene Mutations
Point Mutations (single base change):
• Substitution — wrong base swapped in
• Insertion — base added (causes frameshift ⚠️)
• Deletion — base removed (causes frameshift ⚠️)

Frameshift mutations are MOST DEVASTATING — shifts the entire reading frame!
🔀 Meiosis vs Mitosis
🔄
Mitosis (PMAT)
• Purpose: Growth & repair
• Result: 2 identical diploid (2n) cells
• Where: Body (somatic) cells
• NO genetic variation
• 1 division
🎲
Meiosis (PMAT I + PMAT II)
• Purpose: Sexual reproduction
• Result: 4 unique haploid (n) cells (gametes)
• Where: Gonads (testes/ovaries)
• Creates genetic variation via crossing over
• 2 divisions
🧠 Meiosis Mnemonic
"My (Meiosis) Girls (Gametes) Have (Haploid) Lots (4 cells)"
Meiosis → Gametes → Haploid → 4 cells
⚡ ULTRA SPEED CHEAT SHEET
Helicase
Enzyme that UNZIPS DNA helix during replication
DNA Polymerase
Builds new DNA strand (only 5'→3' direction)
RNA Polymerase
Builds mRNA during transcription
Codon
3-base mRNA sequence = 1 amino acid
Anticodon
3-base sequence on tRNA, complementary to codon
Intron
Non-coding sequences (cut OUT during splicing)
Exon
Coding sequences (KEPT → expressed)
Operon (prokaryotes)
Group of genes controlled by one promoter (e.g., lac operon)
Karyotype
Image of all chromosomes in a cell arranged in pairs
Epistasis
One gene masks the expression of another gene
Polygenic Inheritance
Multiple genes control one trait (e.g., skin colour, height)
Test Cross
Cross unknown dominant phenotype with homozygous recessive (bb) to find genotype
